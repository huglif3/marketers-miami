Day 01 · April 20 Ecosystem
Dinner

Twelve Friends.
One Table.

Intimate Pre-Event Dinner

The week opened with an intimate table. We hosted twelve of our closest builder friends for a deliberate pre-dinner — a choice to create genuine connection before the broader event began. The format was conversational, the atmosphere relaxed, and the conversation substantive.

This was not a networking function in the conventional sense. It was a deliberate gathering of people who are building in parallel, and who benefit from knowing each other. The dinner created the relational foundation that made the next two days more meaningful for everyone in attendance.

Day 02 · April 21 Community
Day

Open.
Exploratory. Real.

Developers · Founders · Operators

The first full day was oriented toward the broader community: developers, founders, operators, and builders exploring or already embedded within the Stellar ecosystem. The energy was open and exploratory, with a strong emphasis on learning and dialogue.

Presentations from Victor Hernandez and Brandon Rodriguez grounded the day with clear technical and strategic context. Talks from Alfred and the teams at Bitso, Stellar, and Etherfuse brought depth across multiple perspectives — from established infrastructure players to emerging protocol-level builders.

What stood out was the coherence across speakers: a shared belief in Stellar as serious, and reliable. New projects entering the ecosystem added a distinct layer of energy — builders working within guardrails, building compliant rails, products built with real users in mind.

Day 03 · April 22 Institutional
Day

The Room
Changed.

Institutions · Capital · Senior Decision-Makers

Day three shifted the register significantly. The room changed composition — institutional organizations, serious capital, and senior decision-makers filled the space, reflecting Stellar's positioning as a platform that has moved well beyond its early adopter phase.

The defining moment of the day — and arguably of the week — was listening to Denelle Dixon speak. Her clarity, composure, and genuine optimism about the trajectory of Stellar were reassuring. She spoke from conviction and deep institutional knowledge.

What resonated most was the accessibility of Stellar's executive team. There was no performance of distance or hierarchy — just a team genuinely engaged with the ecosystem they're building. They closed with an executive lineup open for questions from the room. For a LATAM founder deciding where to build, the signal that leadership is present, reachable, and invested is not a small thing.

OUR PROGRAMMING

2 Speaker Sessions · Founder-Focused Workshops
Session 01 Marketing 101:
For Blockchain Founders Brandon Rodriguez · Etherfuse

Great ideas and even strong products often fail to gain traction in the early stages. Being technically strong is rarely enough. This session broke down a practical go-to-market approach for small teams looking to break through the noise, gain focus, and prioritize scarce resources.

Sharpen Positioning Improve Messaging Distribution Thinking Resource Prioritization

Brandon leads Marketing at Etherfuse, the global leader in tokenizing sovereign debt. Former Paystand, Reach, Visa, Samsung, Intel.

Session 02 Community Is Not
a Channel Victor Hernandez · Plume Network

This session challenged web3's most common assumption — that community is a growth channel. Drawing from building 1M+ member communities at BNB Chain, Bitso, and Plume entirely pre-product, Victor introduced a framework built around Mission, Belonging, and Momentum.

The core argument: most web3 communities fail because they're built on incentives instead of identity. The startups that win treat community not as a channel to broadcast into, but as a culture to build from day one.

Mission Belonging Momentum

8+ years in Web3. Grew Plume Network X to 1M+ followers pre-product. Expanded BNB Chain LATAM community 150% across 6 countries. Built Bitso organic channels from scratch to 30k members.
