Events as a GTM Engine

The CDMX edition was an experiment in collaboration with a local university, Mexico’s leading media team, and an emerging venture studio. It was built to connect and educate the next generation of founders, growth operators, and institutional fintech leaders, while creating strong post-event follow-up potential for Stellar and partner ecosystems.

The event worked best when it felt practical, operator-led, and distribution-aware. The main friction points were show rate, timeliness in planning and programming.

This page reflects the internal debrief notes for the June 17, 2026 CDMX event and includes strategic follow-up observations, warm intro candidates, and recommendations for the 2026 series.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

What happened

MARKETERS IN THE ARENA CDMX brought together a high quality room. The strongest signal was not scale, but density: founders, operators, institutional fintech teams, and ecosystem builders who were materially relevant to Stellar, stablecoins, and GTM in LATAM.

Timing mattered. Running the event the day after Stablecoin Conference helped capture people already in Mexico City, while Espacio Cripto content capture extended the event into a post-event distribution channel through interviews, clips, and organic social posts (coming soon).

WHAT WORKED

Positive signal
Room Quality The room skewed founder-heavy and operator-centric, with strong representation from LATAM ecosystem builders and institutions including Grupo Salinas, BANORTE, and Mercado Pago.
Strategic Timing Holding CDMX immediately after Stablecoin Conference captured attendees who were already in-market and ready for a more curated operator room.
Content Capture Espacio Cripto interviews and speaker clips created a post-event content runway instead of leaving the event value trapped in the room.
Organic Social Proof The #EnLaArena hashtag picked up organically across attendee posts, with real-time tags to @StellarOrg and @myosin_xyz that extended the event’s visibility beyond the room.
Satisfaction Among post-event responses, 13 of 18 attendees gave the event a 5/5 rating, representing roughly 72% top-box satisfaction among respondents.
Partner Discovery Partner networks including Espacio Media, BAF, and Universidad de la Libertad helped drive high-intent discovery rather than generic attendance inflation.

Key Feedback

Signals from the room, timing, and post-event feedback.

LATAM Ecosystem Density The room included founders and operators from BANORTE, Mercado Pago, Bitso, CNBV, and Grupo Salinas alongside crypto-native builders — a cross-section rarely seen at web3 events.
Timeliness and Team Readiness Earlier confirmation of participating teams is essential for producing a stronger program. Knowing which teams will be present allows for better session curation, gives CMOs time to prepare for the Marketing Roast, and improves the overall attendee experience. With only four days between team confirmations and the event, it was difficult to plan effectively.
Stablecoin Week Timing Strategic overlap with Stablecoin Conference drove organic CDMX-based attendance and social amplification during the same week.
Content Format The agenda leaned too heavily on keynotes and firesides. Attendees responded best to execution-layer signal and wanted more hands-on workshop output, not more top-down talking.
Speaker Access Attendees wanted speaker contacts and social handles shared during or immediately after sessions. That should be built into slides, event pages, and printed materials.
Show Rate Approved registrations still outpaced attendance materially. Recommendation: tighten approvals, send a 48-hour confirmation nudge, and consider a more active waitlist flow.
Venue Fit Some feedback questioned whether Universidad de la Libertad was the best location given the distance, though many still pointed to its production quality as a strong upside. This is worth revisiting as the series sharpens its physical-world identity.
