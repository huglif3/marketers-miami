Event Overview
Where marketing
meets what's next.

Marketers in the Arena is a curated, application-gated gathering for the builders, strategists, and operators shaping the next chapter of marketing — across tech, crypto, and the creator economy.

This isn't a conference. There are no booths, no panel marathons, no name-drop keynotes. MITA is a room designed for signal over noise — lightning talks without slides, live anonymous Q&A, and the kind of conversations that only happen when the right people are in the same space.

The CDMX edition lands June 17, 2026 at Universidad La Libertad — 100 carefully selected founders, operators, and ecosystem builders who are building in, for, and alongside Latin America.

Why This Event Matters

Latin America is one of the fastest-growing markets for Web3 adoption, stablecoin usage, and DeFi. Mexico City sits at the center of that moment — a city with one of the most active startup and crypto communities in the region, underserved by events that actually go deep.

MITA CDMX is the missing room. Not a side event. Not a satellite. A first-class gathering for the people doing the serious work — and a cultural moment built around the founders, not the sponsors.

Target Room Composition

We design the room intentionally. Target mix for CDMX: 50%+ founders and operators, 20% developers and protocol builders, 15% investors and BD, 15% marketing and content ecosystem. Every application is reviewed for this balance.

The Event Experience
Culture,
not conference.

MITA runs on a simple editorial philosophy: every element of the event is designed to remove friction between people and real conversation. No panels reading bullet points. No booths. No small talk for small talk's sake.

Lightning Talks,
No Slides
8–12 minute practitioner talks — unfiltered, direct, slide-free. No decks means no hiding behind polished narratives. Just what you actually know. Talks are documented and released as YouTube episodes after the event.
The Arena Wall
Live anonymous Q&A projected in real time. The most honest questions in the room, asked without social risk. Speakers answer in the moment. The tension is the point.
Marketing Hackathon
Teams of 1–4 work two live sprints — research then campaign — and pitch on stage. $3,000 in prizes. Optional but electric. Teams form on the day.
Sponsor 1:1s,
Not Booths
Partners get curated, scheduled 1:1 time with attendees — not a table to stand behind. More valuable, more honest, and more likely to actually result in something.
Post-Event
Working Groups
Select attendees are invited into follow-up sessions around specific themes — turning a one-day event into an ongoing collaboration node for the ecosystem.
Bilingual by Design
English and Spanish throughout — programming, registration, communications, and signage. Designed for a Latin American audience, not adapted for one after the fact.
Programming Themes
The conversations
worth having.

MITA CDMX is organized around five programming tracks, each designed to produce useful signal — not inspiration theater. Speakers are practitioners, not pundits. Talks are specific, not abstract.

Track 01
Growth Without Guardrails
How do you market something the mainstream doesn't understand yet? Distribution strategy, community flywheels, and narrative frameworks for Web3 and emerging tech in LATAM.
GTM
Track 02
The LATAM Advantage
What does it mean to build for this market — not just in it? Localization, remittances, stablecoin adoption, and the cultural nuances that global playbooks consistently miss.
Regional
Track 03
On-Chain Economics for Operators
DeFi mechanics translated for marketing and product leaders: token incentives, liquidity, RWA integration, and the GTM implications of on-chain growth metrics.
Web3
Track 04
Brand in the Age of Protocol
When your product is a protocol, your community is your brand team, and your narrative is open source — how do you build identity that actually compounds over time?
Brand
Track 05
The Founder's Marketing Playbook
Tactical, honest, practitioner-led. What actually worked, what failed publicly, and the frameworks that survive first contact with a skeptical market.
